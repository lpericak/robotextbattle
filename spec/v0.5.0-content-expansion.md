# v0.5.0 Content Expansion — Items, Enemies, Upgrades (Levels 0–50)

## Overview

Expand from 7 weapons / 13 gear / 3 consumables / 6 enemies to a full
progression spanning levels 0–50. Redesign armor into a smooth tier
system. Add new weapons at every stage. Add 11 new enemies. Add
"Armor Plating" upgrade. Allow enemies to have upgrades.

No new mechanics — just more content using existing systems.

## Design Principles

- **Two players**: Oliver (8) and Lucas (6). They love destruction,
  explosions, lasers, and things that go boom. Names should be fun.
- **Smooth curve**: each tier has a "fair fight" enemy, an upgrade weapon,
  and better armor. No dead zones where you grind with nothing to buy.
- **Inventory pressure**: with 4–6 slots, players make real tradeoffs
  (armor vs battery vs extra weapon vs consumable).
- **Dual-wield path**: extra arms + 1-hand weapons is viable alongside
  big 2-hand weapons. Both should scale.

## Armor Redesign

Remove Wooden Armor. Replace with a smooth 8-tier armor line.
Each is a separate gear item (different names, no stacking restriction
on *different* armors, but inventory limits keep it sane).

| Name | Level | HP | Def | Cost | Notes |
|------|-------|----|-----|------|-------|
| Cardboard Armor | 0 | +5 | 0 | $100 | Starter (unchanged) |
| Tin Armor | 2 | +10 | +1 | $250 | Early upgrade |
| Iron Armor | 5 | +20 | +2 | $500 | Mid-early |
| Steel Armor | 8 | +40 | +4 | $1,000 | Mid game |
| Titanium Armor | 12 | +60 | +8 | $2,500 | Late-mid |
| Diamond Armor | 20 | +100 | +12 | $6,000 | Late game |
| Plasma Shield | 30 | +200 | +20 | $15,000 | Endgame |
| Cosmic Armor | 45 | +400 | +35 | $50,000 | Final tier |

Players sell their old armor to fund the next tier. Defence matters
more at higher levels because enemy damage is huge — without it,
even 400 HP evaporates in 1–2 hits.

### Weapon design philosophy

- **1-hand weapons** (Stick, Wrench, Shock Rod, Shotgun, Laser Gun,
  Chainsaw, Thunder Hammer, Death Ray, Antimatter Blade): lower damage
  but usable with extra arms for multi-weapon loadouts.
- **2-hand weapons** (Sword, Flame Thrower, Lightsabre, Rocket Launcher,
  Plasma Cannon, Missile Launcher, Nuke Launcher, Galaxy Destroyer):
  higher raw damage per slot.
- **Energy costs scale** — batteries are essential for heavy weapons.
  Lucas mode ignores this entirely (that's the point).

### Enemy upgrades

Enemies can now have upgrades listed in `enemies.json`. The
`createEnemyRobot` function copies these to the Robot's `upgrades`
array and calls `applyAllUpgrades()`.

Add `upgrades` field to Enemy type (defaults to `[]`).

## Progression Flow

### Early game (Lv 0–5)
- Start with Stick + $100
- Beat MiniBot ($50) → buy Wrench or Cardboard Armor
- Beat Buzzblade ($60) → save for Sword or Tin Armor
- Beat Sparky ($100) → gear up with Shock Rod + Tin Armor
- Beat Rustclaw/Firebot → Iron Armor, better weapons

### Mid game (Lv 5–12)
- Flame Thrower / Shotgun era
- Battery management matters (Shock Rod → Laser Gun)
- Voltank is a skill check (shotgun + armor)
- Boombox has Grenade — consumable threat
- Omega is the first real boss fight (Lightsabre + Steel Armor)
- Save up for Armor Plating upgrade ($5,000)

### Late game (Lv 12–30)
- Rocket Launcher / Chainsaw / Plasma Cannon era
- Ironclad introduces enemy upgrades (armor-plating)
- Laserface is a dodge/accuracy check (dual lasers + EMP)
- Thunderbot/Megacrusher are gear checks
- Inventory 5 upgrade ($5,000) becomes essential
- Missile Launcher / Diamond Armor tier

### Endgame (Lv 30–50)
- Death Ray / Nuke Launcher / Galaxy Destroyer
- Doombot → Nightmare → Apocalypse → TITAN
- Massive HP pools, huge damage, upgrades matter
- Inventory 6 upgrade ($20,000) helps
- TITAN is the ultimate challenge — Galaxy Destroyer + Cosmic Armor + stacked gear
